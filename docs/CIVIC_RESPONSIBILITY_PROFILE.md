# Humanity Union Civic Responsibility Profile

## Purpose

The Civic Responsibility Profile defines what a Member voluntarily takes responsibility for inside Humanity Union. It captures declared scope, priorities, capacity, and notification preferences so the platform can align opportunities, tasks, and alerts with each Member's chosen civic commitments. This document specifies the conceptual model and fields that govern responsible participation—not passive browsing or generic interest alone.

## Core Idea

Humanity Union should not only ask Members what they are interested in.

It should ask:

**"What are you ready to take responsibility for?"**

Interest indicates attention; responsibility indicates willingness to act, respond, and remain accountable within declared limits of time, geography, and capability. The Civic Responsibility Profile makes that distinction explicit and operational across notifications, recommendations, and the private Civic Dashboard.

## Responsibility Scope

The Member selects one primary scope or a defined combination according to platform policy:

- Community
- City
- Region
- Country
- World

Scope limits which content, institutions, notifications, and recommendations are prioritized. Broader scope does not imply unlimited obligation; time commitment and notification preferences bound practical responsibility.

## Priority Categories

Priority categories represent thematic areas the Member is ready to take responsibility for. Examples include:

- Human Security
- Responsibility
- Conscious Existence
- Human Resources
- Our Life
- Environment
- Education
- Democracy
- Healthcare
- Animal Welfare
- Emergency Response
- Local Community

Members may select multiple categories. The platform uses these selections to filter recommendations, surface relevant democratic instruments, and align responsibility-based notifications.

## Priority Tools

Priority tools are the platform capabilities the Member commits to using as part of their civic responsibility:

- Proposals
- Petitions
- Polls
- Voting
- Comments
- Volunteering
- ACTUC
- Institutions

Tool selection indicates willingness to act through specific channels. Notifications and dashboard tasks emphasize selected tools rather than the full platform surface area.

## Time Commitment

Time commitment declares realistic availability for civic participation:

- 10 minutes per day
- 30 minutes per day
- 1 hour per week
- custom

The platform uses time commitment to cap notification frequency, batch non-urgent items, and avoid overwhelming Members beyond their stated capacity. Mandatory security, verification, and direct governance notices may still apply regardless of commitment level.

## Notification Logic

Members receive responsibility-based notifications only according to their selected scope, priorities, tools, institutions, locations, and available time.

Notifications must satisfy all applicable profile constraints:

1. **Scope** — activity falls within the Member's responsibility scope or an explicitly included location.
2. **Categories** — content relates to a priority category the Member has accepted.
3. **Tools** — the requested action uses a priority tool the Member has enabled.
4. **Institutions and locations** — where relevant, the actor or subject matches priority institutions or locations.
5. **Time** — delivery respects time commitment and notification preferences, deferring or digesting lower-priority items when capacity is exceeded.

This logic reduces noise, supports sustainable participation, and aligns alerts with declared civic duty rather than generic engagement metrics.

## Civic Dashboard Connection

The Civic Responsibility Profile powers the Member's private Civic Dashboard after login. The dashboard is the operational workspace for responsible participation; it is not publicly visible.

The Civic Dashboard should show:

- personal tasks
- recommended actions
- responsibility-based notifications
- activity statistics
- Social Activity Score
- progress
- badges
- ACTUC participation
- volunteering opportunities

Dashboard content is generated from the profile fields, activity history, and platform rules. Tasks and recommendations must reflect what the Member has declared they are ready to take responsibility for—not unrelated trending activity.

## Public Profile Connection

The Public Profile is separate from the Civic Dashboard and shows only public information approved by privacy rules.

The Civic Responsibility Profile itself remains private. Other Members and the general public do not see declared scope, priorities, time commitment, or notification settings unless the Member explicitly chooses to publish derived public elements such as badges or aggregate statistics. Civic responsibility is expressed through actions visible on the Public Profile, not through exposure of the full profile configuration.

## Strategic Importance

The Civic Responsibility Profile is one of the core innovations of Humanity Union and distinguishes it from ordinary social networks.

Typical social platforms optimize for attention, engagement time, and content consumption. Humanity Union optimizes for declared obligation, accountable participation, and constructive civic outcomes. By asking Members what they are ready to take responsibility for—and routing tasks, notifications, and recommendations accordingly—the platform transforms participation from passive scrolling into structured civic action aligned with human capacity and intent.

The Civic Responsibility Profile transforms passive interest into responsible civic action.
