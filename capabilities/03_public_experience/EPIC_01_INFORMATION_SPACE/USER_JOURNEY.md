# Purpose

Define how different visitors experience Humanity Union **before registration**.

This document describes visitor intentions, movement patterns and experiential principles across the public information space.

It is a user journey document — not an architecture review, not an implementation guide, and not a visual design specification.

It answers:

- who visits Humanity Union in public;
- what each visitor type intends to accomplish;
- how visitors move from first contact toward informed participation — or calm exit;
- what principles govern that movement without pressure or overload.

Reference:

- `capabilities/03_public_experience/EPIC_01_PUBLIC_INFORMATION_ARCHITECTURE/DISCOVERY_SESSION_01.md`
- `capabilities/03_public_experience/EPIC_01_INFORMATION_SPACE/PUBLIC_INFORMATION_MAP.md`

The Public Information Map defines **what exists** in public.

This document defines **how people move through it** — by intention, not by page inventory.

Registration and operational participation remain beyond the primary scope of this document.

Participate appears as the natural outcome of informed choice — not as a default destination for every visitor.

# Visitor Personas

Public visitors arrive with different intentions.

All personas share the same public architecture — World, Country and Region filters, consistent navigation and progressive disclosure.

Personas describe **motivation**, not user accounts.

A single person may behave as different personas across visits.

## Explorer

**Intention:** Browse civic life on the platform without a specific goal.

The Explorer wants to see what Humanity Union is, what is happening, and whether anything resonates — without commitment.

They value calm overview over depth on first contact.

They may change geographic scope to see what differs locally versus globally.

## Researcher

**Intention:** Investigate a specific civic topic, initiative or institutional context.

The Researcher arrives with a question — often from an external link or search result.

They want accurate public information, related context and traceable civic state.

They compare sources, follow cross-links and read detail before forming judgment.

## Learner

**Intention:** Understand how Humanity Union works as a civic platform.

The Learner seeks process explanation, definitions and durable reference material.

They may not be interested in any live initiative today.

They value comprehension of the participation model before any personal involvement.

## Verification Seeker

**Intention:** Evaluate whether public claims on the platform are legitimate and explainable.

The Verification Seeker scrutinizes derived indicators, evidence presentation and platform governance.

They ask: what is recorded, what is derived, what does Humanity Union claim — and what does it not claim?

Trust must be earned structurally — not through rhetoric.

## Future Participant

**Intention:** Prepare to take part in civic action on the platform.

The Future Participant has moved beyond casual browsing.

They understand enough to consider registration.

They seek clarity on what participation means before crossing the public boundary into operational workspaces.

# User Journeys

Each persona follows a primary navigation path defined by **intention**.

Paths use public destinations from the information map — but emphasis remains on what the visitor is trying to accomplish.

## Explorer

```
Arrive with open curiosity
        │
        ▼
Orient — what is Humanity Union? what scope am I viewing?
        │
        ▼
Browse live civic activity at comfortable depth
        │
        ▼
Sample institutions or media if interest emerges
        │
        ▼
Exit calmly — or deepen into Understand when something catches attention
```

**Primary intention flow:** orient → browse → optionally deepen.

The Explorer may never register.

That is a successful visit.

## Researcher

```
Arrive with a specific question or shared link context
        │
        ▼
Land on relevant civic subject matter
        │
        ▼
Read public projection detail — proposal, decision, support, progress as applicable
        │
        ▼
Gather related context — institutions, media, knowledge cross-links
        │
        ▼
Verify derived labels and evidence presentation
        │
        ▼
Exit with understanding — share, bookmark, or move toward Register if participation is desired
```

**Primary intention flow:** question → detail → context → verification → outcome.

The Researcher values traceability over breadth.

## Learner

```
Arrive to understand the platform itself
        │
        ▼
Seek process and vocabulary explanation
        │
        ▼
Learn participation pipeline meaning at public level
        │
        ▼
Optional — view live initiatives as examples of concepts learned
        │
        ▼
Exit informed — may return later as Researcher or Future Participant
```

**Primary intention flow:** comprehend platform → reference → optional examples.

The Learner may spend entire visits without viewing live initiative listings.

## Verification Seeker

```
Arrive skeptical or auditing public legitimacy
        │
        ▼
Inspect what the platform claims publicly
        │
        ▼
Trace derived civic state to explainable sources
        │
        ▼
Review governance, honesty boundaries and non-claims
        │
        ▼
Exit with evaluated trust — accept, reserve judgment, or share findings
```

**Primary intention flow:** audit → trace → evaluate → conclude.

The Verification Seeker must never encounter certification language that overstates platform authority.

## Future Participant

```
Arrive after sufficient public understanding — often via another persona path first
        │
        ▼
Confirm what participation would mean in this civic context
        │
        ▼
Review registration as explicit choice — not implicit browse consequence
        │
        ▼
Register when ready
        │
        ▼
Cross into operational participation — Capability 02 workspace boundary
```

**Primary intention flow:** confirm readiness → explicit register → participate.

The Future Participant must never be rushed through earlier journey stages.

Registration follows understanding.

# Journey Principles

All public visitor journeys conform to one experiential sequence.

```
Discover

↓

Understand

↓

Trust

↓

Register

↓

Participate
```

## Discover

Visitors encounter civic life openly — initiatives, institutions, media and curated entry — without account requirement.

Discovery is invitation, not capture.

## Understand

Visitors comprehend civic meaning through public projections, cross-links and knowledge reference.

Understanding precedes judgment and precedes registration.

## Trust

Visitors evaluate legitimacy through explainable information — derived values labeled, evidence as substantiation, honest platform boundaries.

Trust is structural verification — not marketing persuasion.

## Register

Visitors choose registration explicitly when ready.

Registration is never implied by browsing, scrolling or reading.

## Participate

Registered visitors enter operational Participation workspaces.

Public Experience ends at this boundary.

---

## Humanity Union never pressures visitors into registration

Public Experience must not use:

- interruptive registration prompts on first visit;
- gated reading that hides public information behind accounts;
- urgency framing, moral pressure or engagement gamification;
- false completeness that suggests browsing is insufficient without signup.

Registration follows understanding.

Visitors may exit successfully at Discover, Understand or Trust without ever registering.

Calm exploration is a valid and complete journey.

Capability 02 established that public transparency must not expose individual dignity.

Capability 03 extends that ethic to **visitor autonomy** — society is welcome to observe without being converted.

# Navigation Philosophy

Public navigation supports **calm exploration**.

## Support calm exploration

Visitors choose depth and pace.

Navigation offers stable destinations — not constant redirection toward registration.

Scope changes (World, Country, Region) must feel like filtering — not disorientation.

## Avoid cognitive overload

Primary header destinations remain limited.

Each visit should present one clear hierarchy — not competing menus, pop-ups and parallel paths to the same meaning.

Personas with narrow intentions — especially Researcher and Verification Seeker — must reach their goal without traversing unrelated domains.

## Use consistent page structures

Listing, detail and reference patterns repeat across destinations.

Once a visitor learns one listing layout, every listing layout should feel familiar.

Consistency reduces learning cost across personas and geographic scope.

Navigation explains where civic information lives.

It does not perform civic authority or pressure participation.

---

# Progressive Disclosure

Information depth increases **naturally** — never all at once.

## First contact — orientation only

Home and first landing moments present:

- what Humanity Union is;
- current geographic scope;
- calm entry into discovery;

—not exhaustive platform completeness.

## Discovery — breadth before depth

Listings and curated highlights show what exists.

Summaries precede detail.

Visitors choose what to open.

## Detail — depth on demand

Detail views reveal civic meaning, pipeline stage context, derived indicators and related links.

Supporting explanation expands when the visitor seeks it — trust footnotes, knowledge cross-links, institutional context.

## Trust layer — visible when scrutinized

Derived values, evidence presentation and platform boundaries are readable without hunting.

Verification Seekers find audit paths without a separate product mode.

## Registration — explicit final layer

Registration gateway appears as a clear choice when the visitor is ready — not as the default next scroll action.

Progressive disclosure rules:

- never hide essential public civic information behind excessive clicks;
- never front-load every pipeline stage, metric and governance paragraph on first view;
- expand explanation in place before adding new destinations;
- preserve honest empty states at Country or Region scope — absence is information, not failure.

# Entry Points

Visitors enter from multiple external contexts.

Every entry point must land in the **same public architecture** defined by the Public Information Map.

## Search engines

Visitor intention: find a topic, platform name or public civic record.

Expected journey: land on relevant public detail or listing → orient via consistent chrome → continue as Researcher or Explorer.

Search entry must not bypass trust labeling or present operational workspaces as default public views.

## Shared initiative links

Visitor intention: follow a specific civic subject shared by another person.

Expected journey: land on initiative public projection context → understand stage and public meaning → optional cross-links → trust evaluation → share, exit or move toward Register.

Shared links are primary Researcher and Future Participant entry paths.

## Media links

Visitor intention: follow announcement, recording or publication reference.

Expected journey: land on media item → link to related initiative or institution context → understand subject → exit or deepen.

Media entry supports understanding — it is not the authoritative participation record.

## Knowledge articles

Visitor intention: learn how the platform works or what a term means.

Expected journey: land on reference content → optional live examples → exit as informed Learner or continue to Initiatives browse.

Knowledge entry may never require registration for reading.

## Direct homepage visits

Visitor intention: open Humanity Union with open or returning curiosity.

Expected journey: Home orientation → scope selection if desired → Explorer or Learner path.

Home must not overwhelm with full platform completeness on first screen.

All entry points converge on one navigation model and one information flow — regardless of persona or scope.

---

# Exit Points

Visitors leave public experience for many valid reasons.

Public Experience must support graceful exit at every stage.

## Register

The visitor chooses accountable participation.

Exit from public journey into Registration Gateway — then operational Participation workspaces.

Explicit, informed, unpressured.

## Share

The visitor shares a public link — initiative, media, knowledge or institutional reference — extending discovery to others.

Share increases visibility.

It must not mutate civic state or imply endorsement by the act of sharing.

## Return later

The visitor bookmarks, remembers or receives a link for a future visit.

Navigation stability and scope consistency make return visits predictable.

No account required to return and continue reading.

## Continue reading

The visitor pauses mid-journey — detail page, knowledge article, institutional profile — without registering or exiting the platform.

Session continuity is experiential convenience — not identity capture.

Public Experience never treats non-registration as abandonment failure.

---

# Final Statement

The Public Experience should guide people **naturally** toward informed participation.

Natural guidance means:

- calm discovery first;
- understanding before judgment;
- trust through explainable public information;
- registration only when the visitor chooses;
- operational participation as the accountable next step — not the default public outcome.

Every persona — Explorer, Researcher, Learner, Verification Seeker and Future Participant — must find a dignified path through the same unified public civic space.

World, Country and Region filter what appears.

They do not change how visitors are treated.

Humanity Union creates grounds for trust in public.

It does not pressure society into participation.

Informed participation is the highest journey outcome — not the only successful one.

This document defines visitor experience before registration.

Domain modeling, page design and implementation must conform to these journeys and principles.

This document does not define implementation.
