# 1. Purpose

Define the **architectural interaction model** of Humanity Union Global Experience.

This document explains how visitors **progressively explore** Humanity Union Public Space through **meaningful interactions** — not through directed funnels, engagement mechanics or registration pressure.

**Global Experience guides exploration rather than directing behaviour.**

Every interaction exists to **increase understanding**.

Interactions are architectural commitments about:

- what the visitor may do;
- what changes when they do it;
- what they can learn from the change;
- how they continue or return.

This document does not define UI implementation, animations, frontend behaviour, components or visual design.

# 2. Interaction Philosophy

Interactions should be:

| Quality         | Architectural meaning                                                           |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Voluntary**   | Visitor chooses to act — no forced paths, gates or interruptive demands         |
| **Predictable** | Same interaction produces same class of outcome across scopes and pages         |
| **Reversible**  | Visitor can return to prior scope, page or overview without penalty             |
| **Explainable** | Visitor understands what an interaction does before or immediately after acting |
| **Respectful**  | Interactions honour attention, dignity and autonomy — no dark patterns          |
| **Calm**        | No urgency, streaks, countdowns or moral pressure                               |

**Every interaction should reduce uncertainty rather than create it.**

After interacting, the visitor should know:

- where they are;
- what changed;
- what they learned;
- how to continue;
- how to go back.

Forbidden interaction philosophy:

- engagement maximization;
- registration conversion optimization as primary metric;
- surprise navigation or bait-and-switch scope changes;
- interactions that hide public information until account creation.

Interactions serve **public understanding** — not platform metrics alone.

# 3. Learning Path

The canonical **exploration sequence** for Global Experience interactions:

```
Observe

↓

Understand

↓

Explore

↓

Evaluate

↓

Participate
```

## Observe

Visitor encounters Global Experience — Hero, map, first scan.

**Interaction goal:** notice that civic platform and activity exist.

No commitment required.

## Understand

Visitor reads Context Introductions and Evidence — statistics, pipeline, initiative previews.

**Interaction goal:** grasp structure and scale of public civic activity.

Comprehension deepens — registration does not.

## Explore

Visitor acts — map scope change, header destination, initiative link, related content.

**Interaction goal:** narrow or broaden context deliberately.

Exploration is curiosity-driven.

## Evaluate

Visitor inspects detail, About paths, derived labels, public projections.

**Interaction goal:** judge legitimacy through verification — not slogans.

Trust builds through exploration.

## Participate

Visitor chooses Registration Gateway — optional transition to registration and Workspace.

**Interaction goal:** informed entry to accountable participation.

**Interaction gradually builds understanding rather than accelerating registration.**

Participate is valid.

Stopping at Evaluate is valid.

Skipping to Participate without Observe–Evaluate is architecturally discouraged — not forbidden by account gate, but not designed for.

# 4. Global Experience Interaction Flow

End-to-end interaction flow from first visit through optional participation:

```
Visitor

↓

Global Experience

↓

Geographic Exploration

↓

Initiative Exploration

↓

Public Understanding

↓

Registration (optional)

↓

Workspace
```

## Visitor

External entry — search, link, direct visit.

**Transition responsibility:** land in unified public architecture — Global Experience or deep link within same template.

No operational workspace as default entry.

## Global Experience

World-scoped public square — observe and understand platform-scale activity.

**Transition responsibility:** orient visitor; offer geographic, statistical and initiative exploration entry points.

Visitor leaves with reduced uncertainty about _what Humanity Union is_ and _that activity exists_.

## Geographic Exploration

Scope transitions — World → Country → Region → future levels.

**Transition responsibility:** change observation filter — not product mode.

Same template, filtered datasets, preserved navigation.

Visitor learns _what matters in this geography_.

## Initiative Exploration

Browse Latest Initiatives, Initiatives destination, initiative public detail.

**Transition responsibility:** move from aggregate evidence to concrete civic paths.

Visitor learns _what specific civic activity looks like_ on the platform.

## Public Understanding

Cross-destination depth — About, Knowledge, Institutions, Media as visitor chooses.

**Transition responsibility:** support evaluation and context — not conversion.

Visitor learns _how platform works_ and _whether claims are bounded and verifiable_.

## Registration (optional)

Registration Gateway interaction — explicit choice.

**Transition responsibility:** cross public boundary to registrant — Capability 01 entry.

Never required to complete prior exploration stages.

Never implied by browse alone.

## Workspace

Operational Participation — Capability 02.

**Transition responsibility:** accountable civic action after registration and governed entry.

Public Experience interaction architecture **ends** at Registration threshold.

Workspace interaction architecture belongs to Capability 02.

# 5. Geographic Exploration

Geographic exploration follows frozen hierarchy:

```
World

↓

Country

↓

Region

↓

Future Geographic Levels
```

Future levels may include:

- District
- Municipality
- City
- Village
- Neighbourhood
- Indigenous Territory
- Additional administrative or community structures

## Interaction model

| Transition       | What changes                                                         | What does not change                                                    |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| World → Country  | Dataset scope; Identity framing; statistics and initiatives filtered | Page template; block types; header destinations; interaction philosophy |
| Country → Region | Finer geographic filter                                              | Architecture language                                                   |
| Any → World      | Broader scope restore                                                | Template and navigation                                                 |

**Every transition changes the scope of observation without changing the architectural language.**

## Primary geographic interactions

- **Geographic Navigator** — explicit scope selection;
- **Interactive Map** — geographic drill-down entry;
- **Scoped URLs or state** — deep link to Country or Region Geographic Experience.

## Reversibility

Visitor returns to World or prior scope without losing navigation context.

Scope change must signal **what changed** — scope label, Identity update, filtered Evidence refresh.

Forbidden: scope transition that reloads different header set or hides primary destinations.

# 6. Information Exploration

Information exploration **narrows context while increasing understanding** — from overview to detail.

```
Overview

↓

Statistics

↓

Participation Pipeline

↓

Initiatives

↓

Initiative Details

↓

Participation
```

## Overview

**Interaction locus:** Global Experience Hero and first scan.

**Understanding gain:** platform identity and observation entry.

## Statistics

**Interaction locus:** Global Statistics block; optional expand or link.

**Understanding gain:** scale of public activity at current scope.

## Participation Pipeline

**Interaction locus:** Initiative Levels block; optional stage filter entry to Initiatives.

**Understanding gain:** structural distribution across pipeline stages.

## Initiatives

**Interaction locus:** Latest Initiatives cards; Initiatives destination listing; map-linked entry.

**Understanding gain:** concrete civic subjects available for depth.

## Initiative Details

**Interaction locus:** Initiative public projection page.

**Understanding gain:** specific civic path — stage, public indicators, evidence summaries, related context.

## Participation

**Interaction locus:** Registration Gateway; governed workspace entry from detail where policy permits.

**Understanding gain:** visitor knows what registration enables — chooses action.

Each step is **optional** — visitor may jump via header, deep link or Related Content — but **presentation order** on Global Experience follows this progression.

Narrowing context:

- World statistics → one initiative → one stage evidence;

Increasing understanding:

- abstract counts → named civic path → verifiable public detail.

# 7. Interaction Principles

The following principles are **frozen** for Global Experience and connected public interactions.

## Every interaction increases understanding

If an interaction cannot articulate what the visitor learns, it should not exist.

## Every interaction has a meaningful purpose

No decorative clicks, empty toggles or engagement-for-engagement patterns.

## No dead ends

Every interaction outcome offers continue, return or cross-link — not blank terminus without orientation.

## Visitors always know where they are

Identity, scope label, breadcrumbs and destination context remain legible after interaction.

## Visitors always know what changed

Scope filter, detail navigation and Evidence refresh explicitly signal change — not silent substitution.

## Visitors always know how to continue

Exploration links, header destinations and Related Content visible from interaction outcomes.

## Visitors always know how to return

Back navigation, breadcrumb ascent, scope reset to World, header Home — reversible paths preserved.

## Interaction follows curiosity rather than forcing progression

No forced linear tutorial, locked content or registration wall on public Evidence.

# 8. Context Before Evidence

Every interaction preserves the pattern:

```
Heading

↓

Context Introduction

↓

Evidence

↓

Visitor Conclusion
```

| Layer                    | Interaction role                                     |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Heading**              | Visitor identifies interaction subject               |
| **Context Introduction** | Visitor understands why evidence below matters       |
| **Evidence**             | Visitor receives public facts and projections        |
| **Visitor Conclusion**   | Visitor judges — platform does not conclude for them |

Interactions that reveal new Evidence — scope change, detail navigation, expand — must preserve Context Introduction before new Evidence layer.

**Humanity Union presents evidence.**

**Visitors form conclusions independently.**

Interactions must not insert platform verdicts, certification copy or "trust us" messaging at conclusion layer.

Registration Gateway is **invitation** — not platform conclusion about visitor judgment.

# 9. Navigation Behaviour

**Navigation supports learning.**

**Navigation never interrupts understanding.**

## Primary navigation broadens exploration

Header destinations — Home, Initiatives, Institutions, Media, Knowledge, About — move visitor across civic domains.

Primary navigation changes **destination** — not architectural language.

Visitor expects new destination responsibility — not surprise content type.

## Related navigation deepens exploration

Related Content, Related Initiatives, Related Knowledge — contextual depth within or across destinations.

Related navigation narrows **context** within learning path.

## Registration Gateway appears only after meaningful understanding

On Global Experience, Registration Gateway interaction sits after Evidence blocks in narrative and template order.

Forbidden navigation behaviour:

- registration modal on arrival;
- header Registration competing with Initiatives;
- interruptive overlay before Context Introduction read path;
- navigation that hides scope change.

Maximum three logical transitions between destinations under normal browse — preserved from Navigation Architecture.

# 10. Trust Through Interaction

Interactions build trust by exposing:

| Trust surface                          | Interaction exposing it                               |
| -------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Observable activity**                | Map, Latest Initiatives, initiative detail navigation |
| **Transparent statistics**             | Statistics interaction with derived labels            |
| **Public initiatives**                 | Initiative exploration path                           |
| **Connected civic spaces**             | Header and Related navigation across destinations     |
| **Verification instead of persuasion** | About entry, detail traceability, honest empty states |

**Trust is strengthened through exploration rather than requested through messaging.**

Trust interactions:

- drill from aggregate to detail;
- follow Related Content to verify context;
- read About without registering;
- inspect derived labels;
- change scope and observe consistent architecture.

Forbidden trust interactions:

- "verify trust" buttons that assert certification;
- pop-ups requesting belief;
- hiding contradictory or sparse data;
- engagement scores as trust proxy.

# 11. Future Evolution

Future interaction capabilities **integrate without architectural redesign**.

Reserved examples — **not authorized in Epic 02**:

| Future capability                | Integration pattern                                                          |
| -------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Advanced Search**              | Exploration stage — footer or Knowledge extension first; preserves template  |
| **AI Discovery Assistant**       | Explain-only orientation — never decides, records or registers for visitor   |
| **Bookmarks**                    | Visitor convenience — does not gate public Evidence                          |
| **Personal Collections**         | Post-registration or optional — never required for public read               |
| **Personalized Recommendations** | Exploration aid — must not replace Related Content civic relationship labels |
| **Live Civic Activity**          | Evidence refresh — same blocks, live dataset                                 |
| **Future geographic levels**     | Geographic Navigator filter extension                                        |
| **Future public capabilities**   | New Evidence blocks — same interaction flow                                  |

Future interactions must preserve:

- Learning Path sequence ethics;
- Interaction Principles Section 7;
- Context Before Evidence;
- voluntary, reversible, explainable interaction philosophy.

Extension adds interaction **capabilities** — not replacement interaction **architecture**.

# 12. Relationship with Capability 02

| Capability                            | Defines                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Capability 02 — Participation**     | Civic participation — aggregates, operational workspaces, public projection sources |
| **Capability 03 — Public Experience** | Civic understanding — Public Space, interaction toward comprehension                |

**Interaction Architecture connects public observation with voluntary participation.**

Connection points:

- Latest Initiatives → initiative public detail → optional Registration Gateway;
- public projection Evidence → visitor evaluation → optional Workspace entry;
- share links → return to public observation path.

Capability 02 does not define Global Experience interaction flow.

Capability 03 does not mutate Participation state through public interactions.

Neither capability replaces the other.

Public interactions are **read-oriented** until Registration Gateway.

Workspace interactions are **participation-oriented** after governed entry.

# 14. Final Statement

**Global Experience interactions are designed to help people understand society before choosing whether to participate.**

Humanity Union measures success **not by the number of clicks**, but by **the growth of public understanding**.

Every interaction should leave visitors **better informed than they were before** — whether they register, explore further, share a link, or leave calmly.

Interactions are:

- voluntary;
- predictable;
- reversible;
- explainable;
- respectful;
- calm.

Geographic exploration changes scope — not architecture.

Information exploration narrows context — increasing understanding.

Registration remains optional invitation — not interaction destination by default.

Workspace awaits — it does not pull.

This document does not define UI implementation.

This document does not define animations or frontend behaviour.

It defines **interaction architecture** for Global Experience and its public connections.

Implementation follows this model.

Interaction architecture never follows engagement metrics alone.
