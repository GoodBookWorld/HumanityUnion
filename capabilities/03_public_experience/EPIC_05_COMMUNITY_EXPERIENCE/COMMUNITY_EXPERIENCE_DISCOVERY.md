# 1. Discovery Purpose

Initiate the **architectural discovery** of **Community Experience** within Humanity Union Public Space.

**Community Experience is the public civic space of one participant-created community.**

It is **not** an independent platform.

It is **not** a separate product, site or navigation universe.

It is the **community-scoped expression** of Humanity Union Public Space — architecturally named **Community Experience at Community scope** — the final public observation level before visitors enter personal Workspace.

Community Experience represents **participant-created communities** rather than **administrator-defined administrative structures**.

Epic 04 delivered Region Experience at Region scope and frozen **Community Discovery** as the handoff from regional geographic observation toward participant-named civic context.

Epic 05 discovery asks how the **same frozen architecture** expresses observable civic reality when the visitor's scope is one **participant-named community** — and how **Find Your Community** enables discovery without replacing organic community creation.

**Community Experience is the final level of Public Experience before visitors enter their personal Workspace.**

This document defines the **architectural role** Community Experience must fulfill before vision, content architecture, interaction architecture, page template standard, architecture review and implementation planning begin.

This document records discovery intent only.

It does not define implementation.

It does not authorize code, routes, components, visual design or Capability 02 backend changes.

# 3. Architectural Questions

Discovery must explore and later answer the following questions — without prescribing implementation.

## What distinguishes Community Experience from Region Experience?

| Dimension            | Region Experience                               | Community Experience                                      |
| -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Scope type**       | Geographic/administrative filter within country | Participant-named civic context                           |
| **Primary question** | What is happening in this region?               | What is happening around this community?                  |
| **Identity subject** | Region within country                           | Community Name and Description — participant-created      |
| **Discovery entry**  | Community Discovery — association browse        | Find Your Community — participant name search             |
| **Map**              | Regional Interactive Map required               | No map required in Version 1                              |
| **Creation model**   | Geographic metadata from public-safe sources    | Communities emerge through Initiative creation and naming |

Community Experience differs in **civic context meaning** — not in page anatomy, trust model or interaction philosophy.

## Why is Community Experience centered on communities rather than administrative units?

Administrative regions describe **where** civic activity may be filtered geographically.

Communities describe **who and what shared civic purpose** participants name when they act together — cities, neighbourhoods, environmental societies, cultural groups, organizations and cause-based collectives.

Humanity Union must reflect **real civic life** without forcing society into rigid administrative categories.

Centering Community Experience on **participant-created communities**:

- honors organic civic naming through participation;
- allows Nelson, Kootenay Lake Protection Society and Nelson Community Garden to coexist as distinct contexts;
- separates geographic observation (Region) from participant-named local context (Community);
- aligns Public Space with Capability 02 Initiative creation reality.

Discovery must not reintroduce administrator-imposed community taxonomies as architectural requirement in Version 1.

## How does Community Experience prepare visitors for Workspace?

Community Experience is the **final public observation level**.

Workspace is the **first personal operational level**.

Discovery must resolve:

- how Registration Gateway on Community page relates to Identity and Workspace entry;
- whether calm **Workspace link** copy is permitted after community observation — distinct from registration pressure;
- how public reading remains complete without account before Workspace transition;
- how Community Experience explains **observation ends, accountable participation begins** without gating public Evidence.

Public Space enables **observation**.

Workspace enables **personal participation**.

Community Experience completes local public understanding so Workspace entry is **informed** — not **surprised**.

## How should Find Your Community operate?

Per `COMMUNITY_CONTEXT_DECISION.md` and Epic 04 freeze — Find Your Community belongs **exclusively to Community Experience**.

Discovery must explore:

- **Find Your Community as block 1** on Community landing or discovery surfaces — search participant-created Community Name and Description;
- relationship between Find Your Community and **direct deep link** to one Community Experience page;
- search behaviour when visitor already arrived from Region **Community Discovery** association;
- empty, sparse and duplicate-name result presentation — honest, non-fabricated;
- linkage to Initiative creation association when participant registers civic path;
- voluntary, explainable interaction — no forced community selection before reading elsewhere on platform.

Find Your Community is **search** — Region Community Discovery is **association browse**.

Both serve discovery — neither replaces the other.

## Which blocks remain identical in pattern to Region Experience?

Per composition priority in Community Context Decision — adapted at community scope:

| Order | Block                            | Community adaptation                                               |
| ----- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1     | Find Your Community              | **New at Community scope** — primary discovery; not on Region page |
| 2     | Community Identity               | Hero · participant-named community Identity                        |
| 3     | Community Statistics             | Statistics — community-filtered aggregates                         |
| 4     | Community Participation Pipeline | Initiative Levels — community-filtered stage distribution          |
| 5     | Latest Community Initiatives     | Latest Initiatives — community-associated public examples          |
| 6     | Registration Gateway             | Participation — calm invitation; optional Workspace entry emphasis |
| 7     | Footer                           | Supporting Navigation — unchanged                                  |

Global chrome unchanged: **Header**, **Geographic Navigator**, **Footer**.

Block **responsibility** pattern is identical.

Block **Evidence** is filtered to community association.

## Which data continues to originate from Capability 02 public projections?

| Block                                     | Projection source                                                      |
| ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Community Statistics                      | Participation Public Statistics Projection — community filter          |
| Community Participation Pipeline          | Participation Pipeline Public Projection — community filter            |
| Latest Community Initiatives              | Initiative Public Projection — community association filter            |
| Community members (public aggregate only) | Only where projection rules explicitly permit — never private identity |

Capability 03 **orchestrates and filters**.

Capability 03 **does not** access operational aggregates or workspace internals.

Capability 02 **owns** Community record truth, association rules and projection builders.

# 4. Visitor Perspective

## Why would a visitor open a Community Experience page?

Typical intentions:

- understand civic activity **around a community I belong to, support, or am researching**;
- move from regional geographic context to **participant-named local civic context**;
- discover **what initiatives this community leads or hosts** through public projections;
- evaluate whether structured participation exists **in this named civic context**;
- follow **Community Discovery** from Region Experience, **Find Your Community** search, Initiative public detail association, or shared community URL;
- prepare for **personal participation** in Workspace after sufficient public observation.

Community Experience serves **local civic context within unified architecture** — not exit from Humanity Union.

## What questions should it answer?

Community Experience should naturally answer:

| Question                                     | Architectural responsibility                                                                |
| -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Where am I?**                              | Community Identity — name, Description, scope label, navigator context                      |
| **What is this community?**                  | Community Identity and Context Introduction — participant-named context, not admin taxonomy |
| **What is happening around this community?** | Evidence — statistics, pipeline, initiatives                                                |
| **How active is this community?**            | Statistics and pipeline — honest aggregates; sparse states permitted                        |
| **What initiatives does it lead?**           | Latest Community Initiatives — concrete public examples                                     |
| **How may I participate?**                   | Registration Gateway — optional; Workspace entry after observation                          |
| **How do I find other communities?**         | Find Your Community — when on discovery surfaces                                            |

These implement the Five Visitor Questions from `PUBLIC_PAGE_TEMPLATE_STANDARD.md` at **community scope** — with **what is happening around this community** as the defining observation question.

## How should Community Experience feel different from Region Experience?

| Region Experience feel                                       | Community Experience feel                                      |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Geographic — administrative or regional place within country | Civic — participant-named shared purpose                       |
| Broader local filter                                         | Named local context                                            |
| Community Discovery prepares descent                         | Find Your Community enables search; Identity completes context |
| Map-centric geographic Evidence                              | Statistics, pipeline and initiatives without required map      |
| "What is happening in this region?"                          | "What is happening around this community?"                     |

**Architectural language identical.**

**Civic meaning different.**

Visitor should feel: **same platform, deeper participant-named local context** — not a new product.

# 5. Community Philosophy

Discovery confirms the following **community philosophy** for Community Experience.

**Communities emerge through participation.**

They are **not predefined** by platform administrators as the sole source of civic local context.

**They represent shared civic purpose** — visible through public projections of Initiative-associated activity.

Communities may be — non-exhaustively, illustratively:

| Form                                       | Examples                                        |
| ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Geographic**                             | city, town, village, neighbourhood              |
| **Organizational**                         | public organization, NGO with civic activity    |
| **Environmental**                          | watershed society, conservation group           |
| **Educational**                            | learning collective, civic education group      |
| **Cultural**                               | cultural association, community arts group      |
| **Professional**                           | professional civic network with public activity |
| **Volunteer**                              | volunteer collective                            |
| **Other participant-created civic groups** | any participant-named shared-purpose context    |

**Do not create fixed Community type taxonomy in Version 1.**

Community philosophy aligns with frozen principle:

**Communities are discovered through participation, not predefined by administration.**

| Principle                       | Community Experience expression                                  |
| ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Organic growth**              | New Community records through Initiative creation need           |
| **Public discoverability**      | Find Your Community searches participant-created names           |
| **Honest presentation**         | Observable activity or honest sparsity — no fabricated vibrancy  |
| **Governance without rigidity** | Platform policy may moderate abuse — without mandatory type enum |
| **Connected, not isolated**     | Ascent to Region, Country and World preserved                    |

Community Experience **names how local society participates** — not how administrators partition a map.

# 6. Community Identity

Discovery explores architectural representation of Community Identity **without changing the page template pattern**.

All items below belong to **Identity, Context Introduction and Evidence layers** — not persuasion or ownership claims by Humanity Union.

## Community Name

- **Architectural role:** primary Identity label — participant-provided public identifier.
- **Discovery questions:** How is duplicate or similar names handled in search and Identity display? How is moderation governed without type taxonomy?
- **Constraint:** name supports **which community context** — not platform certification of legitimacy.

## Representative image

- **Architectural role:** optional Identity visual context — participant or community-associated imagery where policy permits.
- **Discovery questions:** Is image required or optional? Who may provide it in Version 1? How is misuse governed?
- **Constraint:** orientation — not promotional campaign imagery.

## Description

- **Architectural role:** short public explanation of community civic context — participant-provided at creation.
- **Constraint:** Identity layer and Find Your Community search target — not substitute for initiative text or statistics.

## Activity Area

- **Architectural role:** separate discovery filter dimension — thematic or domain classification **distinct from Community naming**.
- **Constraint:** must not duplicate keywords already present in Description or initiative text without additive discovery value.
- **Discovery questions:** Where does Activity Area appear on Community page — Identity adjunct, filter chip, or browse metadata only?

## Community statistics

- **Architectural role:** Statistics block Evidence — Participation Public Statistics Projection at community association scope.
- **Constraint:** derived values labeled; honest zero or low activity; no engagement scoring or community ranking.

## Community initiatives

- **Architectural role:** Latest Initiatives Evidence — Initiative Public Projection entries associated with community.
- **Constraint:** read-only cards linking to public detail — concrete civic paths visitors may trace.

## Community members (public aggregate only)

- **Architectural role:** optional aggregate indicator **only where Capability 02 public projection rules explicitly permit** — e.g. participant count at community scope without identity exposure.
- **Discovery questions:** Is member aggregate displayed in Statistics block, Identity adjunct, or excluded in Version 1?
- **Constraint:** **never private participant identity** — public-safe aggregates only; no roster, no contact data, no operational workspace membership serialization.

**Template pattern unchanged from Region Experience.**

Community Identity adapts **Identity, Context and filtered Evidence** — not page structure.

# 7. Relationship with Region Experience

**Region Experience prepares visitors through Community Discovery.**

**Community Experience becomes the destination.**

## Handoff model (frozen by Epic 04)

```
Region Experience

↓ Community Discovery (association browse)

Community Experience

↓ Find Your Community (name search — when discovery needed)

Community Experience (selected community scope)
```

| Region role                            | Community continuation                         |
| -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Geographic filter complete             | Participant-named civic context applied        |
| Community Discovery lists associations | Community Identity names selected community    |
| Region ≠ Community explained           | Community scope observation begins             |
| Reversible Region ascent               | Community ↔ Region ↔ Country ↔ World preserved |

Region **prepares**.

Community **completes** local public observation.

Community Experience must:

- accept entry from Community Discovery links without requiring Region visit first — deep link valid;
- preserve scope labeling so visitor knows they moved from **geographic region** to **participant-named community** when both apply;
- not re-implement Regional Statistics, Regional Map or regional initiative catalogs;
- not duplicate Community Discovery browse behaviour as Find Your Community search.

Epic 04 freeze: **Community Discovery precedes Community Search** architecturally.

Epic 05 discovery defines **Community Search and Observation** completion.

# 8. Relationship with Workspace

**Community Experience is the final public level.**

**Workspace is the first personal level.**

```
Public Space (Global → Country → Region → Community)

↓

Registration Gateway (optional public invitation)

↓

Workspace (personal operational participation)
```

## Architectural boundary

| Public Space (Community Experience)           | Workspace                                        |
| --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Observable civic activity through projections | Accountable personal operational participation   |
| Read without account                          | Governed authenticated environment               |
| Community-scoped public Evidence              | Initiative workspaces, personal civic action     |
| Humanity Union presents evidence              | Participant acts within Capability 02 governance |

**Public observation ends.**

**Personal participation begins.**

Discovery must resolve:

- Registration Gateway remains **optional** for public reading — unchanged from upstream scopes;
- calm copy may explain **what Workspace enables** after community observation — not urgency or guilt;
- Community Experience does not serialize operational workspace state in public blocks;
- Workspace entry is **governed Identity transition** — not bait-and-switch from public reading.

Community Experience prepares visitors to understand **what this community does in public view** before they choose **what they may do personally** in Workspace.

Stopping at Community observation without Workspace is **valid success**.

# 9. Preliminary Risks

Discovery identifies the following **preliminary risks** — to be addressed in architecture review, not ignored at specification time.

## Duplicate communities

**Risk:** Similar Community Names — "Nelson" vs "Nelson BC" vs "City of Nelson" — confuse search and Identity.

**Mitigation direction:** honest search results with Description disambiguation; governance policy; no forced merge in Version 1 architecture unless policy requires.

## Community fragmentation

**Risk:** Many small inactive communities dilute discoverability and trust.

**Mitigation direction:** honest sparse states; no fabricated activity; Find Your Community ranking must not become engagement optimization dark pattern.

## Inactive communities

**Risk:** Empty Community pages appear abandoned or untrustworthy.

**Mitigation direction:** honest zero-activity presentation; Context Introduction explains sparsity; no pressure to register to "revive" community.

## Search quality

**Risk:** Find Your Community returns irrelevant matches or misses obvious communities.

**Mitigation direction:** search Name and Description only in Version 1 — architecture simplicity; future search epic requires Architecture Review.

## Community ownership assumptions

**Risk:** visitors assume Humanity Union **owns** or **certifies** communities displayed in Public Space.

**Mitigation direction:** Identity and Context copy clarify participant-created context; no platform legitimacy certification language.

## Administrative complexity

**Risk:** pressure to reintroduce administrator community catalog, type taxonomy or approval gates for architectural convenience.

**Mitigation direction:** freeze Version 1 organic model; governance through policy and moderation — not rigid taxonomy; Architecture Review for future catalog features.

## Region vs Community confusion

**Risk:** community named after place overlaps region name — visitor loses scope understanding.

**Mitigation direction:** scope labels visible; Context Introduction explains Region vs Community; Epic 04 handoff discipline preserved.

## Public member aggregate misuse

**Risk:** member counts imply social proof pressure or expose quasi-identifiable small groups.

**Mitigation direction:** projection rules govern display; suppress or threshold small counts; never show identifiable roster.

These risks are **discovery warnings** — not assumed failures.

Architecture review must close them before freeze.

# 10. Success Criteria

Community Experience discovery succeeds when downstream architecture can satisfy visitor understanding of:

| Outcome                                         | Meaning                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **What this community is**                      | Participant-named civic context — Name, Description, optional Activity Area — without admin type taxonomy |
| **What it does**                                | Observable public civic activity through statistics and pipeline — or honest sparsity                     |
| **What initiatives it leads**                   | Latest Community Initiatives — concrete public examples with traceable detail links                       |
| **How active it is**                            | Derived-labeled aggregates — no fabricated vibrancy or ranking                                            |
| **How they may participate**                    | Registration Gateway — voluntary; public reading never gated                                              |
| **How Workspace enables personal contribution** | Calm explanation of transition from public observation to personal operational participation              |

## Immediate familiarity

A visitor who knows Global, Country and Region Experience should **immediately recognize** Community Experience:

- the same block sequence **pattern** and page rhythm;
- the same header and footer;
- the same calm, readable public tone;
- the same Context Before Evidence structure in every block;
- **Find Your Community** as the new primary discovery element at Community scope — expected, not surprising.

## Thirty-second community orientation

Within approximately thirty seconds, a visitor arriving from Region Community Discovery or Find Your Community should state:

**"I am viewing this participant-created community's civic activity inside Humanity Union — and I understand how it differs from the region I came from."**

Thirty seconds measures **community context recognition** — not pipeline mastery or Workspace onboarding.

## Workspace preparation success

Visitor who continues to Workspace should carry:

- understanding of community-scoped public activity observed;
- voluntary choice to participate — not pressured conversion;
- clear distinction between **public Evidence** and **personal operational environment**.

# 11. Final Statement

**Community Experience is the public expression of civic communities created through participation.**

It **completes Humanity Union's Public Experience hierarchy** and **prepares visitors for personal participation within Workspace**.

Community Experience is Community scope — not a separate local portal.

It extends what Epic 04 built through Community Discovery.

It fulfills what `COMMUNITY_CONTEXT_DECISION.md` promised.

It does not replace Region, Country or Global Experience.

It does not fork Public Space architecture.

Downstream artifacts — Community Experience vision, content architecture, interaction architecture, page template standard, alignment with Epic 04 handoff, architecture review, architecture freeze and implementation plan — must conform to this discovery and to frozen Epic 01 through Epic 04 documents.

**No implementation is defined by this document.**

Discovery asks **what Community Experience is for** — and **what architectural questions must be answered** before build authorization.

Implementation waits for architecture.

Architecture waits for honest answers to the questions above.
