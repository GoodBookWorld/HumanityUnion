# Purpose

Define the **architectural vision** of **Community Experience** within Humanity Union Public Space.

Community Experience presents **participant-created civic communities** and their **observable public activity**.

It is **Community scope** — the final public observation level before visitors enter personal Workspace.

This document records **architectural intent** before content architecture, interaction architecture, specification and implementation.

It does not define implementation.

It does not authorize visual design, routing, components or Capability 02 engineering.

# 1. Vision

Community Experience allows visitors to **understand how people unite around shared civic purpose**.

A visitor who arrives at a community — from Region Community Discovery, Find Your Community search, Initiative association or a shared link — does not leave Humanity Union.

They **focus** — applying the same architectural lens to civic activity associated with one **participant-created community**.

**Communities represent real public activity rather than administrative structures.**

Community Experience strengthens **local civic understanding** at the level of **named shared purpose** — cities, groups, societies, organizations and cause-based collectives — without forcing society into rigid administrative categories.

The community view answers a human question:

**"What is happening around this community — within structured civic participation?"**

Community Experience is not a local portal competing with Humanity Union, its region or its country.

It is **one community's window** into the same living society Global, Country and Region Experience reveal at broader scopes.

Visitors may:

- observe community-scoped civic activity in public view;
- understand what this community is and why participants named it;
- see how participation is distributed within the community;
- explore concrete initiatives associated with the community;
- discover other participant-created communities through Find Your Community;
- return to Region, Country and World scope without disorientation;
- choose registration and Workspace entry voluntarily — never as a condition of reading.

Community Experience presents **filtered public projections** — Capability 02 civic truth at community association scope, composed by Capability 03 into the same Experience Block pattern visitors already know from upstream scopes.

It reveals **observable community civic reality**.

It does not promote a community as an organization to join, certify political legitimacy or perform administrative authority.

# 2. Purpose

Community Experience exists to:

## Present community identity

Make **participant-created community context** visible — Community Name, Description and calm Identity framing — without mandatory type taxonomy or administrator-imposed catalog.

## Explain community purpose

Help visitors interpret **why this named civic context exists** through Context Introduction — shared purpose explained, never sold.

## Present community initiatives

Show **concrete public initiative examples** associated with the community — observable civic paths visitors may read without registering.

## Demonstrate observable civic participation

Make structured participation **legible at community scope** — statistics, pipeline distribution and honest sparse states — derived values labeled, no fabricated vibrancy.

## Prepare visitors for Workspace

Complete **local public observation** so optional transition to Registration Gateway and **personal Workspace participation** is informed — not surprised.

**Community Experience explains rather than promotes.**

It orients at participant-named civic context.

It never substitutes marketing for evidence.

It never demands registration to read public information.

Purpose aligns with Epic 01 Public Space model:

**Public Space enables observation.**

**Workspace enables participation.**

Community Experience is the **final public level** before Workspace.

It remains firmly in Public Space until the visitor chooses governed personal entry.

# 3. Guiding Principle

**One Humanity.**

**Many Communities.**

**Shared Future.**

Communities remain **distinct** — named, discoverable, culturally and civically real to participants and visitors.

Communities simultaneously **participate in one connected civic space** — Humanity Union — where structured civic activity may be observed across contexts without architectural fragmentation.

**Communities strengthen Humanity Union by enabling cooperation around shared civic goals.**

| Layer                | Meaning                                                                                                          |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **One Humanity**     | One Public Space architecture, one trust model, one platform identity                                            |
| **Many Communities** | Participant-named civic context — not administrator-only partition                                               |
| **Shared Future**    | Global, national and regional perspective preserved — community view deepens local understanding, not separation |

Geographic scope and civic context change **what data is shown**.

They do not change **what Humanity Union is**.

Community Experience expresses the guiding principle architecturally:

```
Same template pattern

+

Community scope parameter

+

participant-created Identity

=

Community civic observation within unified Public Space
```

Forbidden vision:

- community silos with different navigation;
- community pages that hide Region, Country or World ascent;
- rhetoric that pits communities against Humanity Union as institution;
- administrator-only community directories replacing participation-created growth.

# 4. Visitor Experience

Visitors to Community Experience should understand — naturally, through composition and calm interaction — the following:

## What this community is

**A participant-created civic context** — named and described through participation — not an admin-imposed administrative unit.

Answered by: Community Identity, Description, scope label, Geographic Navigator context where metadata supports ascent.

## Why it exists

**What shared civic purpose participants associated with this name** — explained calmly, not certified by Humanity Union.

Answered by: Context Introduction below Identity and block-level Context Introductions.

## What initiatives it leads

**Concrete public initiative examples** on observable civic paths associated with the community.

Answered by: Latest Community Initiatives, links to public initiative detail.

## How active it is

**What public civic activity is visible at community scope** — or honest sparsity.

Answered by: Community Statistics, Community Participation Pipeline — derived labeling, no ranking.

## How participation remains voluntary

**Registration is optional** — available after sufficient community context, never required to read.

Answered by: Registration Gateway after Evidence sequence — same ethics as upstream scopes.

## How Workspace enables personal contribution

**What changes when observation yields to personal accountable action** — calm explanation, not pressure.

Answered by: Registration Gateway and governed Workspace entry copy — distinct from public Evidence blocks.

Visitor experience preserves Epic 01 journey:

Discover → Understand → Trust → Register → Participate.

Stopping at Understand or Trust is valid.

Community scope **completes public observation**.

It does not **accelerate registration** or **gate public reading**.

# 5. Community Perspective

Community Experience introduces a **community perspective** through filtered public projections — **public civic reality rather than organizational administration**.

Each element below uses the **same architectural block pattern** as Region Experience where applicable.

Only civic context, Identity copy and filtered datasets adapt.

## Community Identity

**Architectural role:** Identity layer — Hero and navigator name the participant-created community; optional restrained visual context where policy permits.

**Vision constraint:** Identity orients — it does not certify community legitimacy, ownership by Humanity Union or organizational authority.

Community Identity answers **what this community is called in public civic space** — not **this organization is officially approved**.

## Community Description

**Architectural role:** participant-provided public explanation of community civic context — Identity and Find Your Community search target.

**Vision constraint:** explains purpose — never replaces statistics or initiative Evidence; subject to public-safe governance policy.

## Activity Area

**Architectural role:** separate discovery filter dimension — thematic or domain classification **distinct from Community naming**.

**Vision constraint:** supports findability without duplicating Description or initiative text keywords; governed vocabulary or list — not participant free-text Identity substitute.

## Community Statistics

**Architectural role:** Statistics block Evidence — Participation Public Statistics Projection filtered to community association scope.

**Vision constraint:** derived values labeled; honest zero or low activity; no community league tables or engagement scores.

Community Statistics quantify **observable public activity** — not community worth or organizational performance ratings.

## Participation Pipeline

**Architectural role:** Initiative Levels Evidence — stage distribution at community scope using **frozen Participation pipeline vocabulary**.

**Vision constraint:** same stages as Global, Country and Region — filter only; no shortened or renamed civic path for community marketing.

Participation Pipeline shows **how participation is structured around this community** — not administrative hierarchy.

## Community Initiatives

**Architectural role:** Latest Initiatives Evidence — Initiative Public Projection entries associated with the community.

**Vision constraint:** read-only cards linking to public detail; no operational fields; recency transparent if curated.

Community Initiatives show **concrete civic paths this community's public activity touches** — not promotional campaigns.

## Community Members (public aggregates only)

**Architectural role:** optional aggregate indicator **only where Capability 02 public projection rules explicitly permit** — e.g. participant count at community scope.

**Vision constraint:** **never private participant identity** — no roster, no contact data, no workspace membership serialization; small-count suppression where policy requires.

Public aggregates support **scale comprehension** — not social pressure or identifiable exposure.

## Find Your Community

**Architectural role:** primary **Community discovery interaction** at Community scope — search participant-created Community Name and Description.

**Vision constraint:** not administrator directory; honest empty results; voluntary and explainable; links to Community Experience at selected scope; supports Initiative association flows — not forced selection before reading elsewhere.

Find Your Community implements:

**Communities are discovered through participation, not predefined by administration.**

**All community perspective elements are filtered public representations** composed into frozen Experience Block pattern.

They are not separate community admin consoles, CRM modules or organizational management surfaces exposed in Public Space.

Capability 02 owns community truth and association rules.

Capability 03 composes **public civic understanding** at community scope.

# 6. Relationship with Region Experience

Community Experience **inherits Humanity Union architecture** from Global, Country and Region Experience.

Epic 04 Region Experience is the **direct geographic parent reference**.

Epic 04 **Community Discovery** is the **regional handoff** — association browse preparing Community descent.

**Community Experience becomes the destination.**

| Region Experience                         | Community Experience                     |
| ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Geographic filter within country          | Participant-named civic context          |
| Community Discovery — browse associations | Find Your Community — name search        |
| Prepares Community descent                | Completes local public observation       |
| Regional Interactive Map                  | No map required in Version 1             |
| What is happening in this region?         | What is happening around this community? |

Community Experience **inherits**:

| Inherited element       | Community behaviour                                                                |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Block sequence pattern  | Find Your Community prepended; Identity through Footer                             |
| Global chrome           | Header, Geographic Navigator, Footer                                               |
| Page template flow      | Identity → Context Introduction → Evidence → Participation → Supporting Navigation |
| Interaction philosophy  | Calm, voluntary, explainable, reversible                                           |
| Trust model             | Projection boundary, derived labeling, honest sparsity                             |
| Registration ethics     | Observation precedes participation                                                 |
| Primary navigation      | Six frozen header destinations                                                     |
| Context Before Evidence | Mandatory on all blocks                                                            |
| Reversibility           | Region, Country and World ascent preserved                                         |

**Only civic context, public datasets and Identity copy change.**

```
Region Experience + community scope parameter + participant Identity → Community Experience
```

Visitors may arrive from Community Discovery — they do not migrate to a different product.

Deep link directly to Community Experience is valid — template preserved.

Region **prepares**.

Community **completes** public observation at participant-named local context.

# 7. Relationship with Workspace

**Community Experience completes Public Experience.**

**Workspace begins personal participation.**

```
Global → Country → Region → Community Experience (Public Space)

↓

Registration Gateway (optional)

↓

Workspace (personal operational participation)
```

## Architectural boundary

| Community Experience                                  | Workspace                            |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Public observation of community-scoped civic activity | Personal accountable civic action    |
| Read without account                                  | Governed authenticated environment   |
| Projection-backed Evidence                            | Operational Capability 02 workspaces |
| Humanity Union presents evidence                      | Participant contributes personally   |

**Observation naturally transitions into contribution** — but only when the visitor **chooses**.

Natural transition means:

- sufficient community context observed first;
- Registration Gateway invitation is calm — not urgent;
- Workspace entry is explainable — not bait-and-switch from public reading;
- stopping at community observation without Workspace remains **valid success**.

Public Space ends before Workspace.

Community Experience is the **last public page class** in the frozen hierarchy.

Workspace is the **first personal environment** — not another public block.

Community Experience prepares visitors to understand **what this community's public civic activity looks like** before they ask **what they may do personally**.

# 8. Architectural Principles

Community Experience conforms to frozen Epic 01 through Epic 04 principles without exception.

## Public Space is the window into living communities

Community pages present **observable community-scoped civic activity** — participant-created contexts visible through public participation projections.

## Observation precedes participation

Registration Gateway follows community Evidence — never opens the community page as a gate.

## Context Before Evidence

Every block at community scope: **Heading → Context Introduction → Evidence → Visitor Conclusion**.

Community Context Introduction explains significance — never persuades.

## Trust Through Verification

Visitors trace community Evidence to initiative public detail and derived labels — same verification path as upstream scopes.

## Explainable Honesty

Community sparse or zero activity shown honestly — not inflated to appear vibrantly active.

## One Experience Block — One Responsibility

Community Experience uses the same block catalog pattern — Find Your Community, Identity, Statistics, Pipeline, Latest Initiatives, Registration Gateway, Footer — no community megablocks merging discovery, Evidence and Workspace entry.

## Communities are discovered through participation

Organic community growth through Initiative creation; Find Your Community searches participant-created names — not administrator catalog monopoly.

Additional binding principle:

| Principle                       | Application                                                                                                               |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Filter Instead of Duplicate** | Community scope filters projections — block responsibilities stay distinct from Region Community Discovery preview fields |

Principles govern tone and honesty.

Template governs sequence pattern.

Community scope governs **civic context filter** — not **architectural exception**.

# 9. Success Criteria

Community Experience vision succeeds when a visitor **immediately understands**:

| Recognition                                            | Expected outcome                                                                            |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **What the community exists to accomplish**            | Shared civic purpose legible from Identity and Description — or honest sparse context       |
| **How it contributes**                                 | Structured participation visible at community scope — pipeline and initiatives, not slogans |
| **How active it is**                                   | Statistics and pipeline honest — derived labeled — or honestly inactive                     |
| **How to explore its initiatives**                     | Latest Community Initiatives and public detail links — without registration                 |
| **How they may begin participating through Workspace** | Calm optional Registration Gateway — Workspace entry explained, not forced                  |

Success within approximately **thirty seconds** at Community scope means:

- the community is named and purpose-oriented clearly;
- community public activity is legible or honestly sparse;
- Region or broader ascent path is obvious where metadata supports;
- Find Your Community role is clear when present;
- registration is not demanded.

Success includes visitors who:

- ascend to Region, Country or World without relearning navigation;
- explore initiatives without an account;
- discover other communities through Find Your Community voluntarily;
- complete public observation without entering Workspace;
- enter Workspace with informed choice after community context.

Failure modes to avoid:

- community page feels like a separate social network or org admin portal;
- header or block pattern changes at community scope;
- community promotional rhetoric replaces Evidence;
- Find Your Community becomes mandatory gate before public reading;
- hidden or broken ascent to broader scopes.

# 10. Final Statement

**Community Experience is the public expression of participant-created civic communities.**

It **preserves Humanity Union's unified architectural language** while providing the **final public step before personal civic participation**.

Community Experience does not fragment Humanity Union.

It **completes** Humanity Union's Public Experience hierarchy — one participant-named community at a time — for visitors who need local civic context within the same public institution Global, Country and Region Experience introduced at broader scopes.

Downstream artifacts — content architecture, interaction architecture, narrative, page template standard, Epic 04 alignment confirmation, architecture review, architecture freeze and implementation plan — must inherit this vision and conform to frozen Epic 01 through Epic 04 documents and `COMMUNITY_CONTEXT_DECISION.md`.

**This document does not define implementation.**

It defines **what Community Experience must mean** architecturally before build authorization.

One Humanity.

Many Countries.

Many Regions.

Many Communities.

Shared Future.

One Public Space — observed globally, understood nationally, explored regionally, **completed communally**, continued personally in Workspace.
