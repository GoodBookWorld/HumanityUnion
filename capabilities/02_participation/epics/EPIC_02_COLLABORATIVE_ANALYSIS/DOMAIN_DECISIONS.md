# DOMAIN_DECISIONS

## Capability 02 — Participation

### Epic 02 — Collaborative Analysis

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

Document the architectural decisions that define the Collaborative Analysis domain.

These decisions explain *why* the domain is designed as it is.

They protect architectural consistency throughout the evolution of the platform.

---

# Decision 01

## Collaborative Analysis replaces Discussion

### Decision

Humanity Union does not implement traditional online discussions.

Instead, every Initiative owns a Collaborative Analysis.

### Rationale

Traditional discussions tend to become:

- unstructured;
- repetitive;
- emotionally driven;
- difficult to summarize;
- difficult to reuse.

Collaborative Analysis encourages structured reasoning instead of conversation.

### Consequences

- Contributions become the primary participation unit.
- Analysis becomes measurable.
- Community knowledge becomes reusable.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 02

## Contribution replaces Comment

### Decision

Participants create Contributions rather than Comments.

### Rationale

A Contribution has an explicit analytical purpose.

A Comment is free-form conversation.

Collaborative Analysis values analytical progress over conversation volume.

### Consequences

Every Contribution belongs to a defined type.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 03

## Contributions are Immutable

### Decision

Published Contributions are never edited.

Corrections are represented by new Contributions.

### Rationale

Historical reasoning must remain transparent.

Knowledge should evolve through additions rather than rewriting history.

### Consequences

- analytical history is preserved;
- Community Memory becomes trustworthy;
- reasoning remains auditable.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 04

## Analysis is Additive

### Decision

Collaborative Analysis grows by adding knowledge.

Existing analytical history is never replaced.

### Rationale

Understanding evolves through accumulation.

Replacing previous reasoning destroys historical context.

### Consequences

The Initiative gains an evolving analytical history.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 05

## Signals complement Contributions

### Decision

Signals never replace Contributions.

### Rationale

Signals provide lightweight analytical feedback.

Meaningful reasoning always requires Contributions.

### Consequences

Signals remain supportive.

Contributions remain primary.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 06

## Progress is Policy-Based

### Decision

Readiness is determined by Progress Policy.

It is not determined by arbitrary voting.

### Rationale

Different Initiatives require different levels of maturity.

Each Initiative may define its own readiness requirements.

### Consequences

Progress becomes transparent.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 07

## Readiness is Measured

### Decision

Readiness is calculated from satisfied requirements.

### Rationale

Participants should understand:

- current progress;
- remaining work;
- blocking requirements.

Readiness should encourage improvement rather than speculation.

### Consequences

Readiness becomes actionable.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 08

## Collaborative Analysis never decides

### Decision

Collaborative Analysis prepares Initiatives.

Poll decides.

### Rationale

Analysis and decision-making are separate responsibilities.

Human governance remains explicit.

### Consequences

The domain preserves Human Leadership.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 09

## Community Memory is external

### Decision

Collaborative Analysis consumes Community Memory.

It does not own it.

### Rationale

Community Memory is a platform service.

Business domains should not own platform infrastructure.

### Consequences

Layer independence is preserved.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 10

## Humanity Intelligence supports analysis

### Decision

Humanity Intelligence may assist Collaborative Analysis.

It never governs Collaborative Analysis.

### Rationale

Intelligence provides support.

Governance belongs to people.

### Consequences

Human authority remains intact.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 11

## Analysis Summary represents understanding

### Decision

Analysis Summary reflects the current analytical state.

It does not replace individual Contributions.

### Rationale

Participants should be able to understand the current state without losing access to detailed reasoning.

### Consequences

Summaries improve navigation while preserving transparency.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 12

## Progressive Maturity

### Decision

Initiatives evolve through progressively higher levels of maturity.

### Lifecycle

Draft

↓

Collaborative Analysis

↓

Ready for Poll

↓

Poll

↓

Ready for Petition

↓

Petition

↓

Ready for Implementation

↓

Implementation

### Rationale

Complex initiatives require progressive validation.

Quality should increase before formal decision-making.

### Consequences

Community effort becomes structured and predictable.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 13

## Collective Intelligence before Collective Decision

### Decision

The platform prioritizes improving collective understanding before asking participants to vote.

### Rationale

Better understanding produces better decisions.

Voting without sufficient analysis reduces decision quality.

### Consequences

Collaborative Analysis becomes a mandatory preparation stage.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Decision 14

## Human Leadership

### Decision

Every significant platform decision remains human.

Collaborative Analysis supports people.

It never replaces them.

### Rationale

Responsibility cannot be delegated to platform intelligence.

### Consequences

The domain remains consistent with Collective Intelligence Foundation.

Status:

Accepted

---

# Final Principle

Collaborative Analysis exists to improve the quality of collective reasoning.

Collective reasoning precedes collective decision-making.
