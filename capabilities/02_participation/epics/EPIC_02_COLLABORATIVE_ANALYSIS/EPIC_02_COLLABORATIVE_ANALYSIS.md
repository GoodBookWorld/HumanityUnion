# EPIC_02_COLLABORATIVE_ANALYSIS

## Capability 02 — Participation

Version 1.0

Status: Architecture Phase

---

# Purpose

Define the Collaborative Analysis capability of Humanity Union.

Collaborative Analysis enables communities to improve Initiatives through structured participation before formal community decision-making.

It replaces unstructured discussions with meaningful Contributions, transparent analysis and measurable progress.

---

# Mission

Transform individual Contributions into structured collective analysis that improves Initiative quality while preserving human leadership.

Collaborative Analysis exists to increase understanding rather than increase conversation.

---

# Problem Statement

Traditional online discussions suffer from common problems:

- unstructured conversations;
- duplicated arguments;
- information overload;
- poor discoverability;
- low knowledge reuse;
- emotional escalation;
- lack of measurable progress.

These problems reduce the quality of collective decision-making.

Humanity Union addresses this challenge through Collaborative Analysis.

---

# Vision

Instead of encouraging people to produce more comments, Humanity Union encourages people to create meaningful Contributions.

Every Contribution should improve either:

- the Initiative;
- the community's understanding;
- the quality of future decisions.

# Scope

This Epic defines:

- Collaborative Analysis domain;
- Contribution model;
- Signal taxonomy;
- Progress Policy;
- Readiness evaluation;
- Analysis Summary;
- Collaborative workspace.

---

# Out of Scope

This Epic does not implement:

- automated governance;
- autonomous decision-making;
- Learning Engine;
- Knowledge Integration Engine;
- automatic Insight generation;
- advanced Humanity Intelligence services.

These remain platform-level responsibilities.

---

# Core Objectives

Collaborative Analysis should:

- improve Initiative quality;
- organize community participation;
- reduce discussion noise;
- encourage evidence-based collaboration;
- support transparent progress;
- prepare Initiatives for Poll.

---

# Core Domain

The primary aggregate of this Epic is:

CollaborativeAnalysis

It manages the collaborative evolution of a single Initiative.

An Initiative owns exactly one Collaborative Analysis.

---

# Core Concepts

Collaborative Analysis includes:

- Contributions
- Signals
- Analysis Summary
- Progress Policy
- Readiness Evaluation
- Analysis Metrics

These concepts work together to improve Initiative maturity.

---

# Contribution Philosophy

Participation is measured by meaningful Contributions rather than message volume.

Every Contribution should have a defined purpose.

Contributions become part of the Initiative's analytical history.

---

# Signals

Signals provide lightweight structured community feedback.

Signals complement Contributions.

Signals never replace reasoning.

---

# Progress Policy

Each Initiative defines its own promotion policy.

Examples include:

- minimum participation;
- support thresholds;
- expert validation requirements;
- regional review requirements.

Progress Policies determine when an Initiative is ready for the next stage.

---

# Readiness

Readiness represents Initiative maturity.

Readiness is transparent.

Participants should always understand:

- current readiness;
- missing requirements;
- remaining work.

---

# Relationship to Poll

Collaborative Analysis never approves Initiatives.

It prepares Initiatives.

Formal community decisions occur during Poll.

---

# Relationship to Collective Intelligence

Collaborative Analysis consumes services provided by:

- Community Memory;
- Humanity Intelligence Layer;
- Intelligence Service Contract.

It does not own these services.

---

# Success Criteria

This Epic is successful when:

- discussions become structured;
- Initiative quality improves;
- duplicate effort decreases;
- evidence becomes easier to discover;
- progress becomes measurable;
- communities reach better-informed decisions.

---

# Future Evolution

Future Epics may extend Collaborative Analysis with:

- automated Insights;
- advanced Recommendation Services;
- Knowledge Integration;
- collaborative summarization;
- intelligent moderation;
- expert routing.

The architectural boundaries defined in this Epic remain stable.

---

# Final Principle

Collaborative Analysis exists to improve collective reasoning before collective decision-making.

Humanity Union values understanding before voting.
