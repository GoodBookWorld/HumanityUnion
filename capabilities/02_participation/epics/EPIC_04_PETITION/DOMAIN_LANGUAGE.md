# Purpose

This document defines the ubiquitous language used throughout the Petition Aggregate.

The language establishes a shared vocabulary for architects, engineers, designers and future contributors.

Petition represents the Public Endorsement stage of the Humanity Union Participation Pipeline.

It transforms an approved Collective Decision into transparent public support and responsible civic participation.

---

# Core Definition

## Petition

A Petition is the Public Endorsement process through which Humanity Union participants and the broader public may express transparent support for an approved Collective Decision.

A Petition is not merely a collection of signatures.

It is the public participation layer connecting Humanity Union with society.

---

# Participation Pipeline

```
Initiative

↓

Collaborative Analysis

↓

Collective Decision

↓

Petition

↓

Implementation
```

Petition begins only after a Collective Decision has been approved.

---

# Public Endorsement

Public Endorsement is the transparent expression of support for an approved Collective Decision.

Support is voluntary.

Support is public by design.

Support contributes to civic legitimacy.

---

# Signature

A Signature represents a participant's explicit endorsement of a Petition.

A Signature:

- belongs to one participant;
- belongs to one Petition;
- is immutable once recorded;
- becomes part of the public record.

Signing does not imply implementation responsibility.

It expresses public support.

---

# Participant

A Participant is a registered Humanity Union member.

Participants may:

- sign Petitions;
- review their participation history;
- withdraw participation only when permitted by Petition Policy.

Participants act within the Humanity Union community.

---

# Public Visitor

A Public Visitor is any person accessing the public Petition page.

Public Visitors may:

- view the Petition;
- read supporting information;
- share the Petition;
- register in order to participate.

Public Visitors cannot sign until they become Participants.

---

# Registration Gateway

Registration Gateway is the transition between public observation and active participation.

Registration enables:

- identity creation;
- transparent participation;
- personal participation history.

Registration is required before signing.

---

# Share Link

A Share Link is a permanent public reference to a Petition.

It allows anyone to access the public Petition page.

Sharing increases visibility.

Sharing does not imply endorsement.

---

# Support Count

Support Count represents the current number of recorded Signatures.

Support Count is derived from recorded participation.

It is never edited manually.

---

# Support Status

Support Status describes the current participation state of the Petition.

Examples:

- Open
- Closed
- Archived

Support Status belongs to the Petition lifecycle.

---

# Petition Policy

Petition Policy defines:

- who may sign;
- signing rules;
- visibility;
- withdrawal policy;
- participation requirements.

Policies are immutable after publication unless explicitly revised.

---

# Community Participation

Community Participation represents support expressed by registered Humanity Union Participants.

Community Participation is informed by:

- Initiative;
- Collaborative Analysis;
- Collective Decision.

---

# Public Participation

Public Participation represents support expressed after public access to the Petition.

Public participants are encouraged to review the supporting materials before signing.

---

# Contribution Recognition

Contribution Recognition acknowledges meaningful participation without evaluating the participant as a person.

The platform recognizes actions.

The platform does not judge personal worth.

Examples:

✔ "Your signature has been recorded."

✔ "Your participation contributes to public support."

✔ "Thank you for supporting this Petition."

The platform avoids statements that evaluate character or assign moral value.

---

# Participant Workspace

Participant Workspace is the personal operational environment of a registered Participant.

Following registration from a Petition, the Participant Workspace presents:

- the originating Petition;
- participation status;
- civic activity statistics;
- recommended next actions.

The workspace supports continued participation rather than ending with a single signature.

---

# Petition Outcome

Petition Outcome summarizes the public support achieved by the Petition.

Examples:

- Active Support
- Support Threshold Reached
- Closed
- Archived

Petition Outcome describes the Petition itself rather than the Collective Decision.

# Final Principle

Petition extends collective decisions into transparent public participation.

Its purpose is not only to collect support, but to strengthen civic responsibility, transparency and constructive engagement between Humanity Union and society.
