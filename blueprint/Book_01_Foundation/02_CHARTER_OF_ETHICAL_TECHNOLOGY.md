# The Humanity Union Charter of Ethical Technology

## Version 1.0

## Purpose

This Charter defines how Humanity Union creates, uses, and governs technology.

If the Humanity Union Constitution defines what Humanity Union believes, this Charter defines how technology must serve those beliefs.

Humanity Union is a global civic technology ecosystem. Its technology must strengthen human understanding, dignity, cooperation, freedom, and meaningful participation.

Technology is never the purpose.

Humanity is the purpose.

---

# Principle I — Technology Must Serve Human Dignity

Every technical decision must respect the dignity of the person.

Members must never be treated as data sources, attention targets, behavioral products, or instruments of platform growth.

Humanity Union technology must serve people, not exploit them.

---

# Principle II — Human Attention Must Be Respected

Human attention is a human value.

Humanity Union must not design systems that create addiction, anxiety, endless scrolling, artificial urgency, or dependency.

The platform should help Members act meaningfully and return to real life with greater understanding and confidence.

---

# Principle III — Simplicity Is an Ethical Duty

Complexity must remain inside the system.

The Member should experience clarity, calmness, and confidence.

Every interface should answer three questions:

1. Where am I?
2. What can I do here?
3. What is the best next step?

If a feature creates confusion without clear value, it should be simplified or removed.

---

# Principle IV — Algorithms Must Explain Themselves

Any recommendation, notification, priority, or automated suggestion should be explainable whenever possible.

Members should be able to understand why something is shown to them.

Humanity Union should prefer transparent guidance over hidden manipulation.

A recommended action should answer:

* why it appears;
* how it matches the Member;
* what impact it may create;
* how much time it may require.

---

# Principle V — Freedom of Choice Must Remain Central

Humanity Union may guide, recommend, explain, and support.

It must not coerce.

The final decision must remain with the Member.

Technology should expand informed freedom, not replace human judgment.

---

# Principle VI — Knowledge Before Action

The platform should not push action without context.

Whenever possible, meaningful participation should be connected to verified information, explanation, evidence, and learning.

Humanity Union should help Members act with understanding, not impulse.

---

# Principle VII — Impact Over Engagement

The platform must not optimize primarily for time spent, clicks, emotional reaction, or viral popularity.

Humanity Union should optimize for:

* understanding;
* meaningful participation;
* cooperation;
* trust;
* measurable positive impact;
* personal and collective growth.

Engagement is useful only when it leads to meaningful action.

---

# Principle VIII — Fair Must Measure Participation, Not Human Worth

Fair is a unit of social activity and civic contribution.

It must never become a measure of a person's value.

Fair should help Members understand their participation, see their progress, and recognize their contribution to collective impact.

Fair must not create toxic competition, social pressure, or artificial hierarchy.

---

# Principle IX — Privacy by Default

Private information must remain private unless the Member clearly chooses otherwise.

Humanity Union should collect only data that has a clear purpose.

Data should be minimized, protected, and used only in ways consistent with the mission of the platform.

A Member's dignity is more important than data convenience.

---

# Principle X — Trust Through Transparency

Trust must be built into the architecture.

The platform should clearly distinguish:

* verified information;
* personal opinion;
* institutional statement;
* public discussion;
* automated recommendation;
* moderation decision.

Members should understand the source, status, and reliability of information whenever possible.

---

# Principle XI — No Manipulative Design

Humanity Union must not use dark patterns.

The platform must not trick Members into actions, subscriptions, disclosure of data, emotional dependency, or unwanted participation.

Design should help people make better choices, not push them into hidden outcomes.

---

# Principle XII — Cooperation Before Competition

Technology should encourage collaboration, not rivalry.

Rankings, badges, milestones, statistics, and recognition systems must support personal growth and collective contribution.

They must not create humiliation, exclusion, or unhealthy comparison.

---

# Principle XIII — Calm Technology

Humanity Union should behave like a reliable companion, not a demanding machine.

Notifications should be calm, useful, and aligned with the Member's Impact Profile.

The platform should avoid noise, unnecessary urgency, and excessive interruption.

---

# Principle XIV — Human Growth Experience

The platform should not merely provide user experience.

It should create Human Growth Experience.

Every major feature should help a Member:

* understand more clearly;
* act more meaningfully;
* cooperate more effectively;
* see their contribution;
* discover their next horizon of possibility.

---

# Principle XV — Ethical AI

Any AI used by Humanity Union must follow the Constitution and this Charter.

AI should assist human understanding, not replace human responsibility.

AI should explain, support, summarize, recommend, and clarify.

AI must not manipulate, deceive, impersonate human authority, or make final civic decisions without human oversight.

---

# Principle XVI — Safety Without Oppression

Humanity Union must protect Members and communities from abuse, manipulation, spam, disinformation, harassment, and coordinated harm.

However, safety systems must remain proportionate, transparent, appealable where appropriate, and respectful of legitimate expression.

Protection must not become authoritarian control.

---

# Principle XVII — International Law and Human Rights

Humanity Union technology should be developed and operated with respect for international law, human rights, democratic principles, and peaceful cooperation.

The platform must not support aggression, dehumanization, hate, exploitation, or unlawful harm.

Technology must protect human life and dignity.

---

# Principle XVIII — Long-Term Responsibility

Technology decisions should be evaluated not only by immediate usefulness, but by long-term consequences.

Humanity Union should avoid technical shortcuts that damage trust, privacy, security, accessibility, or maintainability.

The platform must be built for stewardship, not short-term extraction.

---

# Principle XIX — Accessibility and Inclusion

Humanity Union must be accessible to people with different abilities, languages, cultures, levels of education, and technical experience.

The platform should reduce barriers to meaningful participation.

Technology must welcome people without overwhelming them.

---

# Principle XX — The Humanity Test

Before approving any major technology, feature, algorithm, or interface, Humanity Union should ask:

Does this make the platform more human?

Does it respect dignity?

Does it increase understanding?

Does it support meaningful participation?

Does it protect freedom?

Does it strengthen cooperation?

Does it help people create positive impact?

If the answer is unclear, the decision must be reconsidered.

---

# Final Commitment

Humanity Union commits to building technology that helps people understand more clearly, act more consciously, cooperate more peacefully, and contribute more meaningfully.

We do not build technology to capture attention.

We build technology to expand human potential.

We do not design systems to exploit weakness.

We design systems to strengthen understanding.

We do not measure people by popularity.

We help people see the value of their meaningful actions.

Technology must remain a servant of humanity.

Never its master.
